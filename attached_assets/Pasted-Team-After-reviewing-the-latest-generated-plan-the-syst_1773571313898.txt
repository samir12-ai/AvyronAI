# 1. Content Distribution Logic Must Be Platform-Aware

The current Execution Blueprint generates this structure:

Carousels — 40%  
Posts — 40%  
Reels — 20%

This distribution does not reflect modern platform growth mechanics.

On Instagram and similar platforms, reach and discovery are primarily driven by short-form video. The system must therefore bias distribution toward Reels when the objective is reach or lead generation.

Required correction:

The plan synthesis layer must dynamically adjust content distribution according to:

• Platform algorithm behavior  
• Funnel stage  
• Content objective (reach vs authority vs conversion)

Default distribution should shift toward:

Reels — 60%  
Carousels — 30%  
Posts — 10%

Implementation:

Add a Content Distribution Optimizer step in the Plan Synthesis pipeline that runs after Content DNA generation and before Execution Blueprint construction.

# 2. Budget Feasibility Must Constrain Lead Targets

The system currently calculates:

Expected Leads: 100  
Qualified Leads: 20  
Goal: 20 clients

However the available budget in the plan example is:

$400

The system correctly flags the constraint but still generates a plan assuming the full lead requirement.

This must be corrected.

Required behavior:

Goal Math must enforce budget feasibility constraints before finalizing plan outputs.

The system must calculate:

• Expected CPL  
• Required Ad Budget  
• Traffic Requirement  
• Lead Volume

Then evaluate:

IF Budget < Required Budget

The system must automatically choose one of the following adjustments:

Option A — Reduce lead target  
Option B — Extend timeline  
Option C — Recommend required budget increase

The plan must never assume unrealistic lead volumes without forcing a strategic adjustment.
