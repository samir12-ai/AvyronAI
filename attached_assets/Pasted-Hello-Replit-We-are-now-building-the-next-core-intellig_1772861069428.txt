**Differentiation Engine V3**

This engine must be delivered as a **10/10 production-grade system**, with **strict boundaries**, **zero role overlap**, **high-confidence outputs**, and **very high acceptance criteria**.

* MI v3

**MI v3 → Audience v3 → Positioning v3 → Differentiation Engine V3 → Build The Plan**

---

1. NON-NEGOTIABLE ROLE DEFINITION

---

Differentiation Engine V3 must answer only this question:

**Why is this brand distinct and believable inside the positioning territory already selected?**

It must NOT decide:

* market diagnosis
* competitor intelligence from scratch
* audience segmentation
* pains / objections extraction
* positioning territory selection
* enemy selection
* narrative direction changes
* offer creation
* pricing / packaging / guarantee logic
* channel selection
* content calendar
* posting frequency
* budget allocation
* media buying logic
* final copywriting assets

If the engine starts doing any of the above, it is out of scope and must be corrected.

**Audience v3**

3. REQUIRED OUTPUTS

---

The engine must output only differentiation-level artifacts.

Required output families:

1. **Differentiation Pillars**

   * the core dimensions of distinctiveness

2. **Proof Architecture**

   * what type of proof is required to make the difference believable

3. **Claim Structures**

   * how the brand’s difference can be stated safely and credibly

4. **Authority Mode**

   * how the brand should appear:

     * expert
     * operator
     * transparent guide
     * contrarian specialist
     * niche authority

5. **Mechanism Framing**

   * whether the difference can be expressed through a named framework, process, system, or unique method

6. **Trust Priority Map**

   * which objections must be resolved first to make differentiation believable

7. **Differentiation Confidence**

   * how strong, defensible, and market-relevant the difference actually is

The engine must NOT output:

* new positioning territories
* new audience definitions
* offer designs
* channel plans
* content plans
* ad strategies
* final scripts or copy

---

4. DIFFERENTIATION ENGINE V3 PIPELINE

---

Build this as a **12-layer engine**:

* **10 deterministic layers**
* **1 AI refinement layer**
* **1 validation / stability layer**

Required structure:

**Layer 1 — Positioning Intake Lock**

* Accept only territories already selected by Positioning v3
* Reject any out-of-scope territory
* Enforce dependency correctness

**Layer 2 — Competitor Claim Surface Mapping**

* Extract what competitors are already claiming from MI content DNA / narratives
* Build a claim surface map
* Do not rerun MI logic; only consume MI outputs

**Layer 3 — Claim Collision Detection**

* Detect whether candidate differentiation claims are too similar to competitor claims
* Output collision risk diagnostics

**Layer 4 — Proof Demand Mapping**

* Determine what type of proof is required given:

  * objections
  * awareness
  * maturity
  * territory type

**Layer 5 — Trust Gap Prioritization**

* Rank the trust barriers most relevant to differentiation credibility
* Must use Audience objections as input, not recreate them

**Layer 6 — Authority Mode Selection**

* Select the correct authority expression mode for the territory
* Must not alter positioning direction

**Layer 7 — Proof Asset Architecture**

* Determine what proof categories are needed:

  * case proof
  * process proof
  * transparency proof
  * comparative proof
  * framework proof
  * mechanism proof
  * outcome proof

**Layer 8 — Mechanism Construction**

* Determine whether a unique mechanism / named process / branded method can be supported
* Must not invent offer structure or packaging

**Layer 9 — Differentiation Pillar Scoring**

* Score candidate pillars using:

  * uniqueness
  * proofability
  * trust alignment
  * territory alignment
  * objection coverage

**Layer 10 — Claim Strength Scoring**

* Score each claim structure for:

  * distinctiveness
  * believability
  * defensibility
  * relevance

**Layer 11 — AI Refinement**

* AI may only refine wording of:

  * pillars
  * claims
  * mechanism phrasing
  * authority framing
* AI must not invent new strategy, audience, offer, or execution logic

**Layer 12 — Stability Guard**

* Validate that the final differentiation is:

  * inside the approved territory
  * low collision
  * sufficiently provable
  * trust-aligned
  * objection-aware

DifferentiationScore =

6. HARD GUARDRAILS — ZERO OVERLAP

---

These guardrails are mandatory.

**Guardrail A — No New Territories**
If any layer or AI output introduces a territory not present in Positioning v3, reject it.

**Guardrail B — No Offer Logic**
If output contains:

* pricing
* packaging
* guarantee
* bonus stack
* CTA design
  reject or sanitize it.

**Guardrail C — No Channel Logic**
If output starts deciding:

* Instagram
* ads
* reels
* funnel cadence
* content mix
  reject it.

**Guardrail D — No Audience Recreation**
If the engine starts producing new audience segments or rewriting personas, reject it.

**Guardrail E — No Final Copywriting**
The engine may define claim structures and phrasing logic, but must not produce:

* ad copy
* post scripts
* landing page copy
* full messaging assets

10. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA — VERY HIGH BAR

---

This engine is accepted only if all of the following are true:

1. **No architectural overlap**

   * MI remains MI
   * Audience remains Audience
   * Positioning remains Positioning
   * Differentiation stays inside its role

2. **No dependency drift**

   * The engine cannot run if upstream dependencies are not READY
   * campaignId and snapshot lineage must match

3. **No strategy mutation**

   * The engine cannot invent new territories or rewrite positioning

4. **No offer leakage**

   * The engine cannot design price / package / guarantee / CTA

5. **No execution leakage**

   * The engine cannot decide channels, content cadence, or planning tasks

6. **Claims are evidence-backed**

   * Every accepted claim structure must be supported by trust / proof / objection logic

7. **Collision protection works**

   * Claims too close to competitors must be flagged or rejected

8. **AI remains bounded**

   * AI only refines language, never expands role

9. **Output is usable downstream**

   * Build-The-Plan must be able to consume this engine safely without ambiguity

10. **Test coverage is serious**

* collision tests
