## 1️⃣ Threats Tab Does Not Display Actual Threat Signals

Currently the “Threats” tab displays:

• Evidence Coverage  
• Market Diagnosis  
• Competitor Intent Map  
• Market Trajectory Indices  

However, it does not display explicit Threat Signals, which is the primary purpose of this tab.

Please add a Threat Signals section that lists observed structural risks.

Example format:

Threat Signals

• Narrative convergence increasing across competitors  
• Hook/angle duplication detected in multiple creators  
• Market posting density below category baseline  
• Offer clustering detected in similar price bands  

These must remain observational signals only, not recommendations.

---

## 2️⃣ Market Diagnosis Contains Implicit Prescription Language

Current text example:

"Opportunity for first-mover advantage with consistent presence."

This violates the Signals-Only contract because it contains implied strategic guidance.

Please refactor diagnosis outputs to pure descriptive language.

Correct example:

"Market activity density is currently low.  
Consistent publisher presence is uncommon across analyzed competitors."

Avoid any language implying action or advantage.

Words that must not appear in Layer-0 outputs:

Opportunity  
Advantage  
Focus  
Differentiate  
Establish  
You should / Must / Recommend

## 3️⃣ Market Trajectory Indices Require Signal Interpretation

The following indices are displayed:

• Market Heating  
• Narrative Convergence  
• Offer Compression  
• Angle Saturation  
• Revival Potential  

Currently these appear as raw percentages only, which limits interpretability.

Each index should include a short signal interpretation line.

Example:

Angle Saturation — 59%  
→ Majority of analyzed hooks repeat similar creative angles.

Offer Compression — 6%  
→ Offers remain structurally differentiated across competitors.

This keeps the layer informational without becoming prescriptive.

## 5️⃣ Structural Design Conflict Detected (Layer Overlap Risk)

A potential architectural issue was identified.

Some fields in the Market Trajectory Indices may unintentionally overlap with analysis expected later in the Fortress decision layer.

Example risk:

Indices such as:

• Narrative Convergence  
• Offer Compression  
• Angle Saturation  

If interpreted as strategy guidance, they could influence positioning or creative decisions prematurely, which violates the layer hierarchy.

Reminder of the intended architecture:

Layer 0 — Market Intelligence  
Layer 1 — Fortress (Strategic Decision Engines)  
Layer 2 — Financial Governor  
Layer 3 — Beast (Creative Execution)

To prevent layer leakage:

Market Intelligence must only expose signals, not conclusions that resemble strategy.

Therefore:

• Indices should remain diagnostic signals only  
• No language implying market entry strategy or differentiation paths  
• No ranking of “best approach” or “best angle”

Fortress will later interpret these signals.
