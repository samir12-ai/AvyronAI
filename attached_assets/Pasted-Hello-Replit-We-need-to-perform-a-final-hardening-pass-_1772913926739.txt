Hello Replit,

We need to perform a final hardening pass on MI v3 to fully stabilize the intelligence layer before continuing with the next engines. This adjustment is important because MI feeds Audience, Positioning, Differentiation, and Build-The-Plan, so any misclassification at this level propagates through the entire strategy pipeline.

This request is not about redesigning the system, but about closing the remaining interpretation gaps and ensuring engine consistency without breaking the existing architecture.

Please implement the following improvements carefully.

---

## 1 — Separate Market Activity from Competition Intensity

The current MI interpretation sometimes reports LOW_ACTIVITY / COOLING even in structurally competitive markets.

The issue is that the engine currently evaluates short-term competitor motion (posting changes, CTA intensity shifts, experimentation) but does not separately model structural competition intensity.

Add an internal metric:

Competition Intensity Score

This should consider factors such as:

- number of competitors analyzed
- total content volume across competitors
- engagement density
- narrative diversity
- baseline posting activity

The MI output should distinguish between:

Market Activity → short-term competitor movement  
Competition Intensity → structural crowdedness of the category

Example of correct interpretation:

Market Activity: Low  
Competition Intensity: High  
Trajectory: Cooling

This prevents the system from misclassifying crowded markets as weak.

---

## 2 — Activity Guardrails

Add logical guardrails so the system cannot interpret a market as structurally weak when:

- competitor count exceeds the high-density threshold
- competitors have an established posting baseline

These conditions must enforce a minimum competition intensity floor.

---

## 3 — Demand Signal Stabilization

Demand should not collapse when competitor motion is temporarily stable.

Demand estimation must incorporate:

- engagement density
- audience language signals
- comment activity
- content output patterns
- narrative demand indicators

Competitor inactivity alone must not reduce demand signals to unrealistic levels.

---

## 4 — Competitor Weighting System

Competitors should not contribute equally to market signals.

Introduce a Competitor Authority Weight based on:

- engagement strength
- follower scale
- content output frequency
- dominance indicators

Example weighting tiers:

Small competitor → lower weight  
Mid-tier competitor → medium weight  
Dominant competitor → full weight

This weighting must apply to:

- narrative saturation
- intent detection
- market activity
- dominance analysis

---

## 5 — Sample Bias Detection

Add a diagnostic safeguard that detects when the competitor sample does not represent the market correctly.

Trigger a Sample Bias Warning if:

- the number of competitors is low relative to category size
- authority distribution is highly skewed
- signal density is insufficient

Expose this in diagnostics but do not break the pipeline.

---

## 6 — Real Data Ratio Guard

Synthetic comments are already properly tagged and filtered, but we should add an additional safeguard.

Introduce a minimum real data ratio check before signal extraction.

If the real comment ratio falls below the defined threshold, the system should reduce signal confidence accordingly.

This protects Audience and Positioning engines from synthetic signal leakage.

---

## 7 — Narrative Semantic Clustering

Current narrative saturation relies heavily on pattern detection.

Introduce a lightweight semantic clustering layer so similar narratives expressed with different wording are grouped together.

Example:

“grow faster”, “scale your business”, and “increase revenue quickly” should be recognized as the same narrative cluster.

This will significantly improve saturation detection and opportunity gap accuracy.

---

## 8 — Competitor Lifecycle Detection

Inactive competitors should not distort market activity calculations.

Add lifecycle classification:

Active competitor  
Dormant competitor  
Low-signal competitor

Only active competitors should strongly influence activity scoring.

---

## 9 — MI Diagnostics Transparency

Expose additional telemetry values for internal auditing:

- activityScore  
- competitionIntensityScore  
- demandScore  
- narrativeSaturationScore  
- competitorWeights  
- sampleBiasFlag  
- realCommentRatio  

These diagnostics help verify that MI reasoning is correct.

---

## 10 — Engine Compatibility and Dependency Safety

This hardening must not break downstream engines.

Please confirm compatibility with:

- Audience Engine V3
- Positioning Engine V3
- Differentiation Engine
- Build-The-Plan pipeline

No schema changes should be required.

---

## 11 — Critical Requirement: Engine Connection Refresh

One important issue we have observed previously with updates is that downstream engines sometimes continue reading old snapshot structures or cached outputs.

After this update is implemented, please ensure that:

- all engines reconnect to the latest MI snapshot structure
- dependency readers refresh their references
- no component continues using stale snapshot outputs

This is critical because stale connections can cause silent inconsistencies across the pipeline.

---

## 12 — Acceptance Criteria

This task should be considered complete only when:

- MI clearly separates activity from competition intensity
- demand signals remain stable in crowded markets
- competitor weighting is applied
- sample bias detection is active
- semantic narrative clustering improves saturation accuracy
- lifecycle detection prevents inactive competitors from distorting signals
- diagnostics expose all internal reasoning metrics
- downstream engines correctly read the updated MI outputs
- no cached or legacy snapshot connections remain active
- the full test suite passes successfully

Once these safeguards are implemented and verified, MI v3 can be considered fully hardened and production-grade.
