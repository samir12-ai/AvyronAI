Hi Replit Team,

Before we mark MIv3 as fully closed, there is one remaining architectural requirement that must be addressed.

Currently, several structural signals rely on fixed thresholds such as:

- Narrative convergence > 50%  
- Hook/angle duplication > 40%  
- Offer clustering > 40%  
- Market density thresholds

These static thresholds create a long-term risk because market behavior varies significantly across categories. What appears as saturation in one category may be normal baseline behavior in another.

For this reason, MIv3 must not rely on fixed thresholds for structural pattern detection.

---

## Required Change — Baseline Calibration Layer

Please introduce a baseline calibration mechanism so signals are detected relative to observed market norms rather than hardcoded thresholds.

Instead of rules like:

Narrative Convergence > 50%

The engine should evaluate deviation from a computed baseline, for example:

Observed Narrative Convergence  
vs  
Category Baseline Convergence

Signal detection should then trigger when deviation exceeds a calibrated range rather than a fixed percentage.

Example logic:

Observed Hook Duplication Rate  
compared against  
Baseline Hook Duplication Rate

Signal triggers only when deviation exceeds the expected baseline.

---

## Scope of Calibration

The following indices should be calibrated using baseline comparisons rather than static thresholds:

• Narrative convergence  
• Hook / angle duplication  
• Offer clustering  
• Market density shifts  
• Positioning shifts

This ensures MIv3 remains robust across different industries and prevents false signals caused by category-specific norms.

---

## Expected Outcome

After this change, MIv3 signals should be interpreted as:

Observed Pattern  
vs  
Market Baseline

rather than simply:

Value > Fixed Threshold

This will allow the Market Intelligence layer to adapt automatically to different market structures while maintaining signal integrity.

---

## Final Note

This is the last structural requirement before we close MIv3 as the Market Intelligence layer. Once baseline calibration replaces static thresholds, the engine will be significantly more resilient and category-agnostic.

Please confirm once this calibration mechanism is implemented.
