We are upgrading the Competitive Engine to a performance-driven, dominance-oriented system. This is not a minor refinement — this is a structural intelligence upgrade.

We will build Competitive Dominance Engine v2 with the following architecture:
	1.	Performance-Based Content Selection
For each competitor:

	•	Automatically identify the top 3–5 highest-performing pieces of content (based on views, engagement, comments, shares).
	•	Do NOT analyze random content.
	•	Focus only on proven-performing material.

	2.	Deep Content Dissection Layer
For each selected content piece, extract and structure:

	•	Hook type (question, promise, shock, number, authority)
	•	Offer mechanics (price visibility, urgency, scarcity, social proof)
	•	Psychological trigger (fear, authority, aspiration, result-driven, discount-driven)
	•	CTA mechanics (WhatsApp, DM, form, soft CTA, hard CTA)
	•	Creative structure (format, pacing, framing style)

All outputs must follow a strict schema — no loose summaries.
	3.	Competitive Weakness Detection
For each high-performing content item:

	•	Identify why it works.
	•	Identify what it does NOT exploit (conversion weakness, differentiation gap, pricing opacity, weak CTA, positioning gap).
	•	Output:
	•	Strength exploited by competitor
	•	Weakness not addressed
	•	Opportunity vector

	4.	Dominance Strategy Generator
Instead of copying, the engine must generate an upgraded variant blueprint:

	•	Improved hook logic
	•	Stronger conversion mechanics
	•	Refined positioning
	•	Clear differentiation
	•	CTA optimization
	•	Structural upgrade suggestions (e.g., add proof at second 7, add price transparency test, shift framing angle)

The goal is not imitation — the goal is controlled strategic superiority.
	5.	Controlled Modification Flow
The Competitive Engine must be influential but not autonomous:

	•	It may propose strategic modifications to the Orchestrator plan.
	•	Any modification must require explicit client approval before being applied.
	•	The system must clearly display:
	•	Base Plan
	•	Dominance-Adjusted Plan
	•	Differences between them

No silent overrides.

This engine must feed into the Orchestrator decision layer before execution plans are finalized.

We are not building a competitor mirror tool.
We are building a competitive exploitation engine.

Begin implementation.
