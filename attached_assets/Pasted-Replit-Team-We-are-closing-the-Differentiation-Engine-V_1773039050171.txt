Replit Team,

We are closing the Differentiation Engine V3 phase of the system and need a final hardening pass to ensure the engine is fully stable, logically correct, and free of future architectural conflicts.

The engine is already operational and producing results, but several calibration and boundary protections must be finalized so the system can be considered closed at a 10/10 reliability level.

This request focuses on four areas:

• scoring calibration  
• evidence validation  
• engine boundary protection  
• deployment verification  

The goal is to prevent future logical inflation, false differentiation territories, and cross-engine conflicts with other components of the master architecture.

--------------------------------
1) Uniqueness Score Calibration
--------------------------------

Currently several differentiation pillars return Uniqueness values close to or equal to 100%.

This creates unrealistic differentiation strength and can distort downstream strategic decisions.

Uniqueness must be recalibrated using three weighted inputs:

• narrative distance from competitors  
• rarity of the underlying signal cluster  
• territory saturation across competitors  

Uniqueness should be clamped below 95% so that perfect differentiation is extremely rare.

This prevents artificial inflation and keeps the model aligned with real market conditions.

--------------------------------
2) Positioning Pressure Logic
--------------------------------

Positioning scores currently drop sharply without clearly reflecting competitor strength.

Positioning pressure must be derived from:

• competitor authority weight  
• narrative overlap between competitors and proposed territory  
• market saturation level  

Positioning score should be calculated as the inverse of this pressure so the score reflects true competitive difficulty rather than arbitrary penalties.

--------------------------------
3) Territory Evidence Density Protection
--------------------------------

Differentiation territories must never be created from weak or repetitive evidence.

Before finalizing a pillar, the engine must verify:

• at least two distinct semantic signal clusters  
• evidence coming from at least two different sources (MI, Audience, or Competitor analysis)

If these conditions are not met, the territory must be downgraded to a weak or exploratory signal rather than a confirmed differentiation pillar.

This prevents the system from generating artificial or low-confidence territories.

--------------------------------
4) Mechanism Framing Guard
--------------------------------

The engine currently outputs mechanism types such as "Named Process."

This output must only appear when sufficient structural support exists.

Mechanism framing should require:

• at least two validated claim structures  
• proofability score of at least 50

If these conditions are not met, the mechanism should fall back to a generic differentiation structure rather than presenting a formal methodology.

--------------------------------
5) Engine Boundary Enforcement (Critical)

To avoid architectural conflicts within the master system, confirm that the Differentiation Engine only performs differentiation analysis and does NOT generate strategic decisions.

The engine must remain strictly limited to:

• differentiation territories  
• claim strength analysis  
• proof architecture suggestions  
• mechanism framing recommendations

It must not write or modify outputs belonging to:

• Strategy / Build-The-Plan engine  
• Offer Engine  
• Funnel Engine  
• Financial Governor  
• Execution engines

This boundary must be protected to avoid cross-engine logic contamination.

--------------------------------
6) Conflict Prevention With Downstream Engines

Verify that Differentiation outputs remain advisory inputs only.

Strategy, Offer, and Funnel engines must consume differentiation results but retain full authority over final strategic decisions.

This ensures the system architecture remains layered and modular.

--------------------------------
7) Stability Guard Confirmation

Confirm that Stability Guard continues to fail only for genuine structural issues such as:

• market saturation  
• insufficient demand  
• narrative overlap

Differentiation signal density should not trigger structural instability by itself.

--------------------------------
8) System Deployment Verification (Important)

Please verify that all updates are fully applied across the running environment.

After implementing the changes:

• rebuild the affected modules  
• restart the runtime environment  
• confirm that the live system is running the updated logic

A common issue is implementing code changes without refreshing the running environment, leaving the system executing an older version.

Please explicitly confirm that the updated logic is the version currently deployed and running.
