During the latest MI v3 validation we identified a structural interpretation issue that must be corrected to guarantee analytical reliability for downstream engines (Audience v3, Positioning v3, and the upcoming Differentiation Engine).

The system currently classified the market as LOW_ACTIVITY / COOLING despite the campaign containing 10 competitors in a structurally competitive agency market. Confidence and freshness were correct, which confirms the issue is not data quality but how market activity is interpreted.

The root cause appears to be that the MI engine evaluates short-term competitor motion (posting shifts, engagement volatility, CTA changes, experimentation) but does not separately model structural competition intensity of the category. As a result, a crowded market with temporarily stable competitors can be incorrectly interpreted as a weak or cooling market.

This misclassification propagates risk to downstream systems:
- Audience Engine may underestimate demand intensity
- Positioning Engine may assume artificial whitespace
- Differentiation Engine may build strategies on incorrect market pressure assumptions

To close this permanently, the MI system must distinguish between Market Activity and Market Competition Intensity.

Required Improvements

1. Structural Competition Metric  
Add a persistent internal metric representing Category Competition Intensity based on:
- number of competitors analyzed
- content volume across competitors
- narrative diversity
- engagement density
- posting baseline frequency

This metric should remain stable even if short-term motion is low.

2. Activity vs Competition Separation  
The MI output should represent two different realities:

Market Activity → short-term competitor motion  
Market Competition Intensity → structural market crowdedness

A valid outcome example would be:
Market Activity: Low  
Competition Intensity: High  
Trajectory: Cooling  

3. Trajectory Guardrail  
The trajectory engine must not classify a market as structurally weak if:
- competitor count exceeds the high-density threshold
- content volume indicates active category presence

4. Demand Signal Stabilization  
Demand estimation should not collapse when competitor behavior is stable. Demand should incorporate:
- language signals
- audience signals from comments
- engagement density
- content output

5. Downstream Protection  
Ensure the following engines consume the corrected signal model:
- Audience Engine (signal weighting)
- Positioning Engine (opportunity gap scoring)
- Differentiation Engine (territory validation)

No downstream engine should interpret “low activity” as “low competition”.

6. Diagnostics Transparency  
Expose internal diagnostics in MI telemetry so we can audit:
- activityScore
- competitionIntensityScore
- trajectoryReason
- signalCoverage

7. Regression Protection  
Add validation tests ensuring the following scenario passes:

If competitorCount ≥ high-density threshold AND posting baseline exists →  
competitionIntensity must not evaluate as low even if short-term activity drops.

Acceptance Criteria

The fix is considered complete only when:

- MI correctly separates structural competition from motion
- Markets with many competitors cannot appear structurally weak
- Demand and trajectory calculations remain logically consistent
- Audience and Positioning engines receive corrected signals
- Full regression tests pass without schema changes
- Existing snapshots remain backward compatible

This correction is critical because MI v3 is the foundational intelligence layer feeding the rest of the strategy pipeline.

Please implement the adjustments and confirm once verified.

Thank you.
